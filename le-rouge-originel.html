Le Rouge Originel

Feature Film · In Pre-Production · Summer 2026

A woman embeds herself in the life of a criminal, and slowly becomes capable of sin.

Le Rouge Originel examines proximity as an ethical condition. It asks what occurs when observation ceases to be neutral, and when witnessing becomes participation.

The film unfolds in stillness. Anne does not enter Boris’s world through force or seduction, but through endurance. What begins as a quiet alignment becomes a moral erosion — gradual, nearly invisible — until refusal is no longer possible.

How does a person become capable of sin?

Visually, the film draws from the austerity of Chantal Akerman, the moral architecture of Michael Haneke, and the metaphysical restraint of Krzysztof Kieślowski.

Dialogue is minimal. Duration is deliberate. The frame observes without guiding judgment.
